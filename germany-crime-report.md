## 6. Scientific Perspective: Understanding Crime Data

### Correlation â‰  Causation
Statistically, non-German suspects are often younger and male â€” traits already linked to higher crime risk. Nationality alone is not a causal factor.

### Why Foreign Suspects Appear in Crime Stats
- Higher police attention in migrant neighborhoods
- Increased reporting of migrant-perpetrated crimes
- Demographic concentration in high-crime urban areas

### Reporting & Enforcement Biases
- Over-policing in migrant communities
- Political and media emphasis on non-German suspects

### Scientific Findings & Perspectives
- **Ifo Institute**: No direct link between migration and crime after adjusting for age, gender, and urban density
- **Migration Media Service**: Emphasizes that the stereotype of the "young, male, criminal migrant" is overused and politically charged

**Sources:** Ifo Institute, Migration Media Service, [InfoMigrants](https://www.infomigrants.net), [The Local](https://www.thelocal.de)
